## Call 1: Aetna Quarterly Business Review

**Date:** 2025-11-15
**Payer:** Aetna
**Attendees:** Sarah Friedman (Headway), Tom Briggs (Headway - Provider Ops), Diana Choi (Aetna - Network Strategy), Mark Sullivan (Aetna - Behavioral Health), Karen Whitfield (Aetna - Claims Operations)
**Call Type:** Quarterly Business Review (QBR)

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- Reviewed Q3 performance metrics: claims volume up 11% QoQ, clean claim rate at 93.4%, denial rate holding steady at 7%. Aetna team expressed satisfaction with directional trends.
- Diana raised the topic of expanding Headway's Medicaid coverage footprint in NJ and CT. They're seeing network gaps in those states for outpatient behavioral health and want to explore whether Headway can bring on providers in those geographies by Q2 2026.
- Mark flagged that they've been hearing feedback from their member services team about occasional confusion around provider directory listings, specifically therapists showing as "accepting new patients" when their panels are actually full. Asked for a plan to improve directory accuracy.
- Karen walked through claims processing improvements on their end. They've reduced their internal adjudication timeline from 21 to 18 days and want to align with Headway on clean claim submission best practices to push this further.
- Discussed the upcoming contract auto-renewal in June 2026. Aetna is open to a rate conversation if Headway can demonstrate continued volume growth and quality improvements. No formal ask yet but signaled flexibility.

**Action Items:**
1. Sarah to send NJ/CT provider supply analysis to Diana by 12/1
2. Tom to prepare directory accuracy improvement plan and share with Mark by 12/15
3. Schedule follow-up on claims submission best practices (Karen + Headway claims team) in January
4. Sarah to prepare rate proposal framework for discussion at Q1 2026 QBR

**Sentiment:** Positive - Expansion minded, collaborative tone throughout. Aetna clearly sees Headway as a strategic behavioral health partner.

## Call 2: UnitedHealthcare Escalation Call

**Date:** 2025-12-03
**Payer:** UnitedHealthcare
**Attendees:** David Kowalski (Headway), Emily Tran (Headway - Claims), Robert Okonkwo (UHC - Provider Relations), Janet Li (UHC - Claims Disputes), Steve Brennan (UHC - Network Operations)
**Call Type:** Escalation / Issue Resolution

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- UHC raised concerns about a spike in claims denials in November, specifically for Medicare Advantage members in TX. Denial rate jumped from 9% to 14.2% for that population. Root cause appears to be a coding change UHC implemented on 11/1 that wasn't communicated to Headway's claims team.
- Janet acknowledged the communication breakdown on their end and committed to issuing a retroactive fix for affected claims, estimated at approximately 1,200 claims worth roughly $380K. Timeline for reprocessing: 30-45 days.
- Robert brought up credentialing delays. They have a backlog of 47 Headway providers who submitted credentialing applications more than 60 days ago. Their SLA is 45 days. He committed to clearing the backlog by 12/31.
- Steve mentioned upcoming network adequacy reviews in GA and NC. UHC wants to ensure Headway can meet minimum provider-to-member ratios before they expand the partnership to those states. Needs data on Headway's provider pipeline in those markets.
- David pushed back on the lack of advance notice for the coding change and requested that UHC implement a 30-day advance notification protocol for any billing/coding modifications. Robert agreed this was reasonable and said he'd escalate internally.

**Action Items:**
1. Janet to initiate reprocessing of ~1,200 denied MA claims by 12/10
2. Robert to clear credentialing backlog (47 providers) by 12/31
3. David to provide GA/NC provider pipeline data to Steve by 12/15
4. UHC to propose advance notification protocol for coding/billing changes by 1/15
5. Emily to audit all Nov MA claims in TX to identify full scope of impact

**Sentiment:** Negative initially, improved to Neutral by end of call. UHC acknowledged fault on the coding change. Credentialing delays are a recurring frustration. Expansion opportunity in GA/NC is a positive signal despite the operational issues.

## Call 3: Cigna Partnership Expansion Discussion

**Payer:** Cigna

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- Alan opened by sharing that Cigna is investing heavily in behavioral health digital access for 2026 and sees Headway as a top-tier partner. They're specifically interested in expanding the EAP overflow arrangement to cover all 50 states (currently limited to 12 states).

- Rate negotiation for the upcoming renewal was briefly discussed. Cigna acknowledged current rates are competitive but noted budget pressure. Marcus positioned the quality data as justification for maintaining or improving rates.

**Action Items:**
1. Marcus to draft EAP 50-state expansion proposal with projected volumes by 12/15
2. Priya to prepare formal outcomes report for Cigna C-suite distribution by 1/10
3. Nicole to send API integration requirements doc to Headway product/eng team by 12/8
4. Greg and Marcus to schedule follow-up on EAP step-up pathway design in January
5. Rate negotiation working session to be scheduled for early February

**Sentiment:** Very Positive - Cigna is actively looking to deepen the partnership. Multiple expansion vectors. Strong alignment on outcomes-driven care.

## Call 4: Magellan Health Operational Review

**Payer:** Magellan Health
**Attendees:** Amanda Reyes (Headway), Kevin Park (Headway - Provider Success), Linda Marsh (Magellan - Utilization Management), Doug Patel (Magellan - Network Services), Carla Simmons (Magellan - Quality)
**Call Type:** Monthly Operational Review

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- Linda expressed concern about utilization patterns. Average sessions per member are at 4.8 vs. their expected 3.5. She asked whether Headway has clinical protocols that might be driving higher utilization. Amanda clarified that Headway's measurement-based care approach sometimes indicates longer treatment courses and that outcomes data supports the additional sessions.
- Doug raised issues with the credentialing timeline. Magellan requires recredentialing every 2 years and they're seeing a 15% lapse rate among Headway providers, meaning providers are seeing members after their credentials have technically expired. This is a compliance risk for Magellan.
- Carla reviewed quality metrics. Clean claim rate is at 87%, below the contractual threshold of 90%. She noted that the top issues are incorrect modifier codes and missing prior authorization references. She wants Headway to implement pre-submission claim scrubbing.
- Kevin acknowledged the recredentialing gap and shared that Headway is building an automated recredentialing reminder system that should be live by Q1 2026. He committed to a manual audit of all providers with lapsed credentials by 12/20.
- Amanda discussed the possibility of renegotiating the managed behavioral carve-out terms, noting that the current flat rate doesn't adequately account for the acuity mix Headway is seeing. Magellan was non-committal but agreed to review data.

**Action Items:**
1. Amanda to provide utilization data with outcomes correlation by 12/20
2. Kevin to complete manual audit of lapsed provider credentials by 12/20
3. Headway claims team to implement pre-submission claim scrubbing for Magellan claims by 1/31
4. Amanda to prepare rate renegotiation data package for January discussion
5. Carla to share updated modifier code guidance specific to behavioral health by 12/15

**Sentiment:** Neutral to Slightly Negative - Magellan has legitimate operational concerns (credentialing, claim quality). Not adversarial but clearly holding Headway to account. Relationship needs attention.

## Call 5: Blue Cross Blue Shield Contract Renewal Kickoff

**Date:** 2025-12-10
**Payer:** Blue Cross Blue Shield
**Attendees:** Jennifer Walsh (Headway), Sarah Friedman (Headway - SVP Commercial), Michael Torres (BCBS - VP Network Strategy), Angela Brooks (BCBS - Behavioral Health Director), Ryan Cho (BCBS - Contracting)
**Call Type:** Contract Renewal Negotiation Kickoff

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- Michael opened by stating that BCBS views the Headway partnership positively and wants to renew but needs to see rate competitiveness. They're benchmarking Headway against other behavioral health platforms (Spring Health, Lyra, Alma) and need to ensure value alignment.
- Angela shared member satisfaction data: 81% of BCBS members using Headway report being "satisfied" or "very satisfied" with their experience. However, she flagged that time-to-first-appointment has been slipping, from an average of 5 days to 8 days over the past 6 months, and that this is a key metric for their behavioral health access standards.
- Ryan outlined the contracting timeline: they need a signed renewal by 3/1/2026 to avoid a gap. He presented an initial rate proposal that represents a 2% reduction from current rates, citing market compression and competitive alternatives.
- Sarah pushed back on the rate reduction, presenting Headway's quality metrics, network breadth, and the fact that BCBS's behavioral health claims volume through Headway grew 14% YoY. She proposed a 1.5% rate increase tied to quality thresholds.
- Both sides agreed to exchange detailed data packages and reconvene in early January for substantive negotiation. Jennifer noted that the BlueCard national access component is highly valuable and should be factored into the economic discussion.

**Action Items:**
1. Jennifer to prepare comprehensive value proposition deck with quality metrics, volume trends, and member outcomes by 1/5
2. Ryan to share BCBS's detailed rate benchmarking analysis by 12/20
3. Sarah to model 3 rate scenarios (flat, 1.5% increase, performance-based hybrid) for internal review
4. Angela to provide detailed time-to-first-appointment data by state for Headway's review
5. Next negotiation session scheduled for 1/8/2026

**Sentiment:** Neutral - Professional and constructive but there's real tension on rates. BCBS is applying competitive pressure. Renewal is expected but terms are genuinely uncertain.

## Call 6: Humana Medicare Advantage Check-In

**Date:** 2025-12-16
**Payer:** Humana
**Attendees:** Brian Nakamura (Headway), Lisa Park (Headway - MA Programs), Denise Warren (Humana - MA Behavioral Health), Paul Kim (Humana - Stars Quality), Maria Santos (Humana - Provider Network)
**Call Type:** Monthly Check-In

**Key Topics Discussed:**
- Paul led a discussion on Stars rating implications. Behavioral health is becoming a bigger factor in Humana's overall Stars performance, and they're tracking Headway's contribution to HEDIS measures around follow-up after mental health hospitalization and depression screening. Headway is performing above benchmark on depression screening (88% vs. 82% national avg) but below on follow-up after hospitalization (61% vs. 68% benchmark).
- Denise shared that Humana is seeing increased demand for psychiatry specifically in FL and TX. Wait times for psychiatric prescribers on Headway's network are averaging 14 days, which is too long for their standards. She asked about Headway's psychiatry recruitment pipeline.
- Maria discussed network composition. Humana would like to see more bilingual providers, particularly Spanish-speaking therapists, in FL and TX to serve their Medicare Advantage population. Currently only 12% of Headway providers in those states list Spanish as a spoken language.
- Lisa presented data on Headway's MA member engagement: 67% of members who start therapy complete at least 4 sessions. She proposed a joint pilot for a care coordination program where Headway would proactively reach out to MA members who miss appointments.
- Brian asked about the possibility of expanding the partnership to Humana's commercial book of business. Denise said it's been discussed internally but that they want to demonstrate clear Stars improvement through the MA partnership first.

**Action Items:**
1. Lisa to prepare follow-up-after-hospitalization improvement plan by 1/10
2. Brian to provide psychiatry recruitment pipeline data for FL and TX by 12/23
3. Maria to send bilingual provider requirements and target ratios by 12/20
4. Lisa to draft care coordination pilot proposal for joint review in January
5. Brian to schedule introductory call with Humana commercial team for late Q1 2026

**Sentiment:** Positive - Humana is engaged and collaborative. They see Headway as important to their Stars strategy. Psychiatry supply and bilingual providers are real gaps that need addressing, but the overall tone is partnership-oriented.
