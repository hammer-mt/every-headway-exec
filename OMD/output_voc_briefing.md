# Voice of Customer — Cross-Functional Briefing

**Date:** 2026-03-04
**Sources:** Payer call transcripts, Slack call reports, structured customer feedback themes, payer health scorecard
**Prepared for:** Cross-functional leadership

---

## 1. VoC Summary

- **Payers want to grow with Headway — but operational basics are getting in the way.** Cigna, Aetna, Kaiser, and Humana are all actively pursuing expansion (EAP scale-up, Medicaid footprint, value-based deepening, Stars partnerships), yet credentialing delays, clean claim shortfalls, and directory inaccuracies undermine confidence in our ability to execute at scale.

- **Credentialing is the most widespread operational pain point.** UnitedHealthcare, Magellan, Humana, and Optum Behavioral all raised credentialing timeline or recredentialing lapse concerns. UHC had 47 providers past their 45-day SLA; Magellan flagged a 15% lapse rate as a compliance risk. This is the single issue most likely to damage multiple relationships simultaneously.

- **Access is slipping and payers are watching.** Time-to-first-appointment moved from 5 to 8 days (BCBS data), psychiatry wait times sit at 14 days (Humana), and bilingual provider supply is at just 12% in FL/TX. Multiple payers tie these metrics directly to contract value and renewal willingness.

- **Measurement-based care outcomes are Headway's strongest card.** Cigna called the 23% PHQ-9 improvement "compelling" and wants to present it to their C-suite. Kaiser's value-based contract is beating benchmarks. Outcomes data is the differentiator that justifies rate holds and fuels expansion conversations.

- **Rate pressure is real but navigable.** BCBS proposed a 2% rate reduction; Cigna noted budget pressure; Magellan's flat rate doesn't reflect acuity mix. Headway is countering with quality data, but the window to lock in favorable 2026 terms is narrowing — the BCBS renewal deadline is 3/1/2026.

## 3. Themes by Source

### Credentialing Delays
- **Call Transcripts:** UHC escalation call documented 47 providers past 45-day SLA. Magellan flagged 15% recredentialing lapse rate as compliance risk.
- **Slack Reports:** UHC credentialing appeared in three separate Slack entries (10/12, 11/26, 12/03). Cross-functional impact noted as affecting "provider satisfaction and revenue realization."
- **Structured Feedback:** Rated Critical priority. Mentions from four payers. Status: automation planned for Q1 2026.
- **Consistency:** High — all three sources align on severity and urgency. No tension.

### Clean Claim Rate
- **Call Transcripts:** Magellan cited 87% rate (below 90% contractual threshold) with specific issues: incorrect modifier codes and missing prior authorization references. Aetna at 93.4% and satisfied.
- **Slack Reports:** Magellan clean claim rate appeared in both October (86.5%) and December (87%) entries. Slight improvement but still below threshold.
- **Structured Feedback:** 18 mentions (highest frequency). Mixed sentiment — Aetna positive, Magellan negative.
- **Consistency:** Strong alignment. Notable tension: Magellan's rate improved from 86.5% to 87% between Slack entries, but the structured feedback still categorizes this as "In Progress." The trajectory is positive but too slow.

### Time to First Appointment
- **Call Transcripts:** BCBS stated average wait moved from 5 to 8 days over 6 months. Positioned as a key behavioral health access standard.
- **Slack Reports:** BCBS pre-renewal discussion (11/04) listed this as a concern alongside rate pressure.
- **Structured Feedback:** 12 mentions, Negative sentiment, High priority, status "Under Investigation."
- **Consistency:** Aligned across sources, but "Under Investigation" status is a concern — this was flagged as early as November and should be further along.

### Psychiatry Supply Gap
- **Call Transcripts:** Humana documented 14-day average wait for psychiatric prescribers in FL/TX. Tied directly to Stars rating performance.
- **Slack Reports:** October check-in (10/22) already flagged psychiatry wait times; December entry (12/16) repeated the concern with added bilingual provider dimension.
- **Structured Feedback:** 9 mentions, Critical priority.
- **Tension:** The Slack reports show this issue was raised as early as October, but the structured feedback still lists it as "In Progress — Targeted recruitment in FL and TX." Two months of visibility without resolution suggests a recruiting bottleneck.

### Measurement-Based Care Outcomes
- **Call Transcripts:** Cigna called 23% PHQ-9 improvement "compelling." Kaiser value-based contract beating benchmarks. Humana depression screening at 88% vs. 82% national average.
- **Slack Reports:** Optum, Kaiser, and Cigna all requested MBC data in some form. Kaiser's year-end review (12/18) specifically noted outcomes beating benchmarks.
- **Structured Feedback:** 10 mentions, Positive sentiment, Medium priority.
- **Consistency:** Strong alignment. This is the one area where all sources are uniformly positive. The opportunity is to operationalize this advantage faster — formal outcomes reports, C-suite-ready materials, and embedding it into every renewal and rate conversation.

### UHC Claims Denial Spike
- **Call Transcripts:** Detailed documentation of TX Medicare Advantage denial rate jumping from 9% to 14.2% due to undisclosed coding change. Approximately 1,200 claims worth roughly $380K to be reprocessed.
- **Slack Reports:** December escalation entry captured this with cross-functional impact to Claims, Finance, and Data teams.
- **Structured Feedback:** Rolled into the broader "Claims Processing Speed" theme at 18 mentions.
- **Tension:** The structured feedback somewhat buries the UHC-specific severity by aggregating it with the general claims theme. The $380K reprocessing exposure and communication failure warrant standalone tracking.

## 5. Recommended Actions by Department

### Commercial / Account Management
1. **BCBS Renewal (Urgent — 3/1/2026 deadline):** Finalize value proposition deck with quality metrics, volume trends (14% YoY growth), and member satisfaction (81%). Model the performance-based hybrid rate scenario as the lead offer — it bridges the gap between their 2% reduction and our 1.5% increase. Prepare a walk-away position.
2. **Magellan Relationship Recovery:** Assign a dedicated account lead for weekly check-ins through Q1 2026. Proactively share progress on clean claim rate improvements and credentialing audit results before they ask. The goal is to move sentiment from negative to neutral before any rate renegotiation conversation.
3. **Cigna EAP 50-State Proposal:** Accelerate the expansion proposal. Cigna is the highest-health payer (91) and most enthusiastic partner — capitalize on momentum before budget cycles close. Include projected volumes and operational readiness assessment.
4. **UHC Communication Protocol:** Formalize the 30-day advance notification agreement for coding/billing changes. Document the $380K claims reprocessing as a case study for why this protocol matters. Use it to rebuild trust.

### Product
1. **Directory Accuracy — Real-Time Availability Sync:** Accelerate development. Aetna (our second-highest-health payer) flagged this, and it appeared across 14 feedback mentions from four payers. Ship an MVP that syncs provider panel status at least daily.
2. **Cigna API Integration:** Assign product/engineering resources to scope the digital front door integration once Cigna sends API requirements. This is a strategic integration that would significantly increase switching costs with our top-scoring payer.
3. **Scheduling Capacity Analysis:** Investigate the time-to-first-appointment slippage from 5 to 8 days. Determine whether this is a supply problem (not enough providers) or a distribution problem (providers available but not matched efficiently). BCBS is making renewal decisions based on this metric.

### Operations / Network
1. **Credentialing Automation (Q1 2026 — Confirm On Track):** The automated recredentialing reminder system must launch on schedule. Three payers are raising this as a compliance risk. Magellan's 15% lapse rate could trigger contractual penalties. Confirm engineering timeline and resource allocation.
2. **Magellan Claim Scrubbing:** Implement Magellan-specific pre-submission claim scrubbing by 1/31 as committed. Focus on modifier code errors and missing prior authorization references — the two issues Carla Simmons specifically identified. Target 90%+ clean claim rate by end of Q1.
3. **Psychiatry Recruitment — FL/TX Sprint:** Current 14-day wait time is unacceptable for Humana's Medicare Advantage standards and is blocking commercial expansion. Set a 90-day target to reduce psychiatric prescriber wait times to under 10 days in FL and TX.
4. **Bilingual Provider Initiative:** Scope a targeted recruitment campaign for Spanish-speaking therapists in FL/TX. Current 12% is insufficient for Humana's MA demographics. Set a 6-month target of 20% and coordinate with DEI team.

### Leadership / Executive
1. **Portfolio Risk Review:** Three payers (Magellan, UHC, BCBS) representing significant revenue are at Medium or High risk. Executive sponsors should be assigned to each, with monthly progress reviews through Q1 2026.
2. **2026 Rate Strategy Alignment:** Multiple renewals are converging (BCBS by 3/1, Optum Q1, Cigna February). Finance and Strategy need a unified rate framework that leverages MBC outcomes data consistently across all negotiations. The 23% PHQ-9 improvement stat and 88% depression screening rate should be in every rate deck.
3. **Outcomes Reporting Formalization:** Cigna explicitly asked for a C-suite-ready outcomes report. Kaiser and Humana want similar data for Stars and value-based contract justification. Invest in a standardized outcomes report template that can be customized per payer — this is Headway's primary competitive differentiator against Spring Health, Lyra, and Alma.
4. **Advance Communication Protocol:** The UHC coding change that caused a $380K denial spike happened because of an undisclosed policy change. Advocate for mutual advance notification clauses in all payer contracts going forward. This is a systemic risk, not a one-off.
