## 1. Executive Summary

- **December total payer revenue was $34.3M, down $1.8M (-5.1%) from November's $36.1M.** Every payer declined month-over-month, consistent with typical December seasonality (holiday scheduling, fewer business days). Despite the dip, all payers remained above their implied monthly contract targets.
- **Full-year contract performance is solid across the core portfolio.** Seven of ten payers are at or above 95% of annual revenue targets. Anthem (102.9%), Humana (102.0%), and Magellan (102.7%) have already exceeded their full-year targets.
- **Two contracts require immediate attention.** Optum Behavioral (expires Feb 28, 2026) and Blue Cross Blue Shield (expires Mar 31, 2026) are both in renewal-pending status. Combined, these represent $78M in annualized contracted revenue.
- **Evernorth (Cigna Behavioral) is materially behind pace** at 60.3% of its annual target, though this is a new partnership in a designated ramp period.
- **Q4 2025 closed at $114.3M in total company revenue (+7.0% over Q3)**, with improving claim approval rates and faster payment cycles across the board.

## 6. Key Themes and Recommended Actions

### Theme 1: December Seasonal Dip Is Normal, but January Recovery Must Be Confirmed

Every payer declined 4.6-5.4% from November to December. This aligns with expected holiday-season patterns (fewer sessions, shorter billing month). The forecast projects a rebound in January.

**Action:** Monitor January weekly claims volume by the second week of the month. If the expected 8-12% volume increase does not materialize by mid-January, investigate root causes (provider scheduling, patient no-shows, credentialing delays).

---

### Theme 2: Two Major Renewals Demand Immediate Executive Attention

Optum Behavioral ($32M, expires Feb 28) and BCBS ($46M, expires Mar 31) are both in pending renewal status. Together they represent $78M in annualized revenue, or roughly 19% of total company revenue.

**Action:** Schedule executive-level check-ins with both account teams this week. For Optum, confirm that terms are being finalized and identify any open negotiation items. For BCBS, ensure the rate increase request (1.5-3%) is being pursued and that directory integration compliance is documented. Both renewals should be on the executive dashboard with weekly status updates until closed.

---

### Theme 3: Rising Denial Rates at UHC and Magellan Are Eroding Potential Revenue

UnitedHealthcare Commercial denial rates have climbed from 8% to 10-11% over six months. Magellan has been consistently above 12%. At current claim volumes, UHC denials represent roughly 2,100-2,600 claims/month not being paid; Magellan roughly 750/month.

**Action:** Commission a denial root-cause analysis for both payers, prioritized by dollar impact. Common causes to investigate: incomplete authorization, coding mismatches, timely filing issues, and documentation gaps. Set a 90-day target to reduce UHC Commercial denials below 8% and Magellan below 11%.

---

### Theme 4: Evernorth Ramp Needs Structured Milestones

At $569K/month, Evernorth is running well below the pace needed to reach its $8.5M annual target. While the ramp period extends through Q2 2026, there are no visible interim targets or operational metrics being tracked.

**Action:** Work with Marcus Chen (account owner for both Cigna and Evernorth) to establish quarterly ramp milestones. Request monthly claims and utilization data from Evernorth so the partnership can be monitored with the same rigor as other payers. Clarify the financial terms of the "shared risk corridor" to understand downside exposure.

---

### Theme 5: Expand Forecasting Coverage and Reconcile Data Gaps

Forecast inputs cover only 5 of 10 payers (77% of revenue). Evernorth has no monthly operational data at all. The Q4 quarterly total exceeds payer-level detail by ~$8.9M, suggesting uncaptured revenue streams.

**Action:** (1) Extend the revenue forecasting model to all payers, including Humana, Kaiser, Optum, Magellan, and Evernorth. (2) Add Evernorth to the monthly claims reporting pipeline. (3) Reconcile the $8.9M gap between quarterly totals and payer-level monthly data to ensure no revenue is unaccounted for in operational reporting.
