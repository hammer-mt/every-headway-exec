## 6. Recommendations — Top 5 Recruiting Priorities

### Priority 1: Recruit 4+ Providers Who Accept Medicaid and Medicare
**Why:** Only 12 providers accept Medicaid (9.1%) and 7 accept Medicare (5.3%). This limits access for low-income populations and seniors — two of the highest-need demographics in mental health. In a New York-based network, Medicaid coverage is essential. Target LCSWs and PMHNPs, who are more likely to accept public insurance.

### Priority 2: Recruit 3+ Spanish-Speaking LMFTs Who Offer Family and Couples Therapy
**Why:** The network has 14 LMFTs and 18 Spanish speakers, but only **2 Spanish-speaking LMFTs** (Casey Null and Samuel Freeman). Neither offers couples therapy as a combined service with Spanish fluency and family focus. Given that Hispanic/Latinx communities are the largest non-English-speaking demographic in the NYC metro, and family/couples therapy is in high demand, this is a high-impact gap.

### Priority 3: Recruit 3+ Eating Disorder and Perinatal Specialists
**Why:** Only **2 providers** list eating disorders and **2 providers** list perinatal/postpartum as specialties. Both are rapidly growing demand areas — eating disorders surged post-pandemic, and perinatal mental health awareness is driving referrals. Ideally recruit providers with DBT training (effective for eating disorders) and those who also accept Medicaid (perinatal Medicaid coverage is expanding nationally).

### Priority 4: Recruit 5+ Providers Specializing in Child/Adolescent Therapy
**Why:** Only **13 providers (9.8%)** serve children under 13, and only **2** formally list child therapy. With rising demand for pediatric mental health services, especially ADHD (21 providers list it but few serve young children), autism spectrum (1 provider), and school-related issues (20 providers but limited child age range), this is a critical capacity gap. Prioritize LPCCs and LMFTs with play therapy training.

### Priority 5: Recruit 3+ DBT-Trained Providers and 2+ Gottman-Certified Couples Therapists
**Why:** DBT is available from only 16 providers (12.1%) despite being the standard of care for personality disorders (12 providers list this specialty), self-harm (8), suicidal ideation (11), and emotional regulation (9). The Gottman Method — the leading evidence-based couples approach — has only 8 providers. With 53 providers offering couples therapy, upgrading the couples-therapy evidence base with more Gottman-trained clinicians would strengthen the network's quality positioning.
