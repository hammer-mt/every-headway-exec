### Source Effectiveness

| Source | Applications | Hires | App-to-Hire | Avg TTF | Offer Accept Rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Referral | 1,580 | 41 | 2.59% | 25 days | 91.1% |

Referrals are the standout channel: 2x the conversion of LinkedIn, 40% faster to fill, and an offer acceptance rate 21 points higher than LinkedIn. The Careers Page is effectively broken -- it produces only 8.8% of hires while generating 22.3% of total applications, creating screening overhead with minimal return.

### Top 3 Recruiting Bottlenecks

1. **Engineering top-of-funnel selectivity is too tight.** Only 13.4% of Engineering applicants reach a phone screen -- the lowest of any department. Combined with 3,115 applications over 6 months, the team is drowning in volume but advancing too few candidates. This is the primary driver of the 41-day average time-to-fill.

2. **Careers Page is a dead channel.** Across all departments, the Careers Page converts at 0.61% (vs. 2.59% for Referrals). It also has the lowest offer acceptance rate at 52.6% -- nearly half of candidates who receive offers through this channel decline. Almost half the Careers Page job postings over 6 months resulted in zero hires (blank time-to-fill entries indicate unfilled roles).

3. **Offer acceptance rates for Engineering and Sales lag.** Engineering (72.2%) and Sales (71.4%) lose roughly 3 out of every 10 offers extended. In a 6-month period, that translates to an estimated 15 declined Engineering offers and 8 declined Sales offers -- representing months of wasted sourcing, screening, and interviewing effort.

## Section 5 — Predictive Insights and Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Launch a Sales Retention Emergency Plan (Critical -- This Quarter)

**The problem:** 85.7% of Sales carries elevated flight risk. Four of seven employees are high-risk with engagement scores between 65-71. The department has already lost 2 people in the past 6 months. If 2-3 more depart, Sales capacity drops below the threshold needed to maintain pipeline.

**The action:**
- Conduct stay interviews with all 7 Sales employees within 2 weeks, led by Derek Holloway's manager (not Derek, given the span-of-control issue).
- Benchmark Sales comp against market. Two of the attrition exit reasons cited higher comp elsewhere; Sales' low engagement scores suggest comp may be a root cause.
- Evaluate whether Derek Holloway (the sole Sales manager, M2, himself flagged Medium risk at engagement 78) has adequate support. A 6:1 span with 4 high-risk reports and his own elevated risk score is unsustainable.

**Projected impact:** Retaining even 2 of the 4 high-risk Sales employees avoids an estimated 2-3 months of recruiting time per role (at Sales' 29-day TTF plus ramp), saving $150K-$250K in replacement and lost-productivity costs.

### Recommendation 2: Fix the 6-18 Month Retention Cliff (High Priority -- Next 60 Days)

**The problem:** 70% of departures occur between months 6-18, with IC2s disproportionately affected. Exit interviews cite growth limitations and comp gaps. Headway is investing in onboarding and early development, then losing employees right as they become productive.

**The action:**
- Implement structured career conversations at the 6-month and 12-month marks, with documented development plans and promotion criteria.
- Create an "IC2-to-IC3 acceleration" track with clear milestones, visible to employees from day one.
- Review comp bands for IC2 and IC3 levels against current market rates, particularly for Engineering and Product roles where FAANG and well-funded startups are the primary competition.

**Projected impact:** Reducing 6-18 month attrition by even 30% would retain approximately 2 additional employees per year, avoiding ~$300K in replacement costs (assuming fully-loaded cost of a mis-hire at 1.5-2x salary for technical roles).

### Recommendation 3: Double Down on Referrals, Sunset the Careers Page in Its Current Form (High Priority)

**The problem:** The Careers Page generates 22% of applications but only 9% of hires, with a 52.6% offer acceptance rate and a 43-day average time-to-fill. Meanwhile, Referrals convert at 4x the rate, fill 40% faster, and achieve a 91% acceptance rate.

**The action:**
- Increase referral bonuses by 25-50% and launch a structured referral campaign, targeting Engineering and Sales specifically.
- Overhaul or sunset the Careers Page. Current state creates screening burden (1,649 applications yielding only 10 hires) and likely contributes to recruiter burnout.
- Reallocate Careers Page sourcing effort toward referral enablement, direct sourcing on LinkedIn, and university/bootcamp partnerships.

**Projected impact:** Shifting 20% of Careers Page volume to Referrals would yield an estimated 8-10 additional hires per year while reducing average TTF by 5-7 days and improving offer acceptance rates by 15+ points.

### Recommendation 4: Address Engineering Funnel Velocity Before It Becomes a Headcount Crisis (Medium Priority)

**The problem:** Engineering has the longest time-to-fill (41 days), the lowest application-to-screen rate (13.4%), and the lowest app-to-hire conversion (1.25%). The department also lost 3 employees in 6 months and has 6 more at Medium/High risk. If attrition continues at the current rate while hiring remains slow, Engineering net headcount will decline.

**The action:**
- Audit the Engineering phone screen process. At 13.4% pass-through with 3,115 applications, there may be an over-indexed screening rubric or insufficient recruiter capacity to process volume.
- Explore Engineering-specific hiring tracks (take-home assessments vs. live coding, async interviews for Remote candidates) to reduce the 41-day cycle.
- Assign a dedicated Engineering recruiter if not already in place; at 39 hires in 6 months, this volume warrants specialized attention.

**Projected impact:** Improving screen-to-onsite conversion by 5 points and reducing TTF by 7 days would yield an additional 5-8 Engineering hires per year and reduce time-to-productivity for open roles by approximately 1 month.

### Recommendation 5: Invest in Manager Capacity for Overstretched Teams (Medium Priority)

**The problem:** Multiple departments have IC-to-Manager ratios of 6:1, and one voluntary departure explicitly cited manager relationship issues. Sales, People, and Clinical Ops each have a single manager supporting 6 ICs. In Sales specifically, the sole manager (Derek Holloway) is himself flagged Medium risk, creating a cascading failure scenario if he departs.

**The action:**
- Promote or hire a second manager in Sales within the next quarter. The current structure is untenable with 4 high-risk reports.
- Evaluate whether Brandon Williams (People HRBP, IC3, Medium risk, engagement 77) and his manager relationship needs attention -- People already lost one IC2 citing manager issues.
- Build succession plans for all single-manager departments, identifying high-performing ICs who could step into M1 roles if needed.

**Projected impact:** Reducing manager span in Sales from 6:1 to 3:1 directly addresses the engagement and development gaps driving flight risk. Industry data suggests that improving manager-to-IC ratios in high-risk departments reduces voluntary attrition by 15-25%.
