## 2. Tradeoff Analysis

### Scenario A: Conservative

| Gains | Risks |
|-------|-------|
| Preserves internal pay equity across IC4 Product cohort | Candidate ask is met on base but total package is thin — may feel like a lowball when compared to competing offer's likely total comp |
| Lowest budget impact; leaves room for future merit increases | High probability of decline: the $7K base premium over the competing offer is marginal, and a Series C company likely has meaningful equity upside |
| Sets no precedent for inflated offers at this level | If the candidate declines, we restart a search that has already consumed recruiting cycles |

### Scenario B: Competitive

| Gains | Risks |
|-------|-------|
| Market-aligned base eliminates the "underpaid" narrative from day one | $7K above candidate ask on base — candidate may anchor future expectations at above-ask offers |
| Signing bonus provides a tangible "win" for the candidate without inflating ongoing comp | Equity grant at $90K is above midpoint; if applied broadly, raises the equity budget for IC4 Product hires |
| Clear total comp premium over the Series C competing offer, making the decision straightforward for the candidate | Moderate budget impact, though within band |
| Strongest acceptance probability without overpaying | — |

### Scenario C: Aggressive

| Gains | Risks |
|-------|-------|
| Virtually guarantees acceptance — removes any financial reason to decline | Creates internal equity pressure: existing IC4 Product Managers in New York earning $185K–$195K may feel undercompensated |
| Sends a signal that Headway competes aggressively for growth-critical talent | $110K equity grant at band max leaves no room for equity refresh increases in Year 2 |
| Positions Headway as a top-of-market employer for Product roles | Candidate may be "bought" rather than mission-aligned — retention risk if another aggressive offer appears in 12–18 months |
| — | Sets a precedent that competing offers trigger top-of-band packages |

## 3. Patterns from Recent Offers

### Acceptance Rates by Positioning

| Positioning | Offers | Accepted | Acceptance Rate |
|-------------|--------|----------|-----------------|
| At or below candidate ask | 9 offers (O-4002, O-4004, O-4006, O-4007, O-4009, O-4010, O-4011, O-4013, O-4015) | 9 | **100%** |
| Below candidate ask with structural sweeteners | 4 offers (O-4001, O-4003, O-4005, O-4012) | 1 | **25%** |
| Significant gap to candidate ask | 2 offers (O-4008, O-4014) | 0 | **0%** |

**Key finding:** Every offer where the base salary met or came close to the candidate's ask was accepted. Declines clustered where the base fell meaningfully short of expectations, even when equity and signing bonuses were included.

### Candidates Lost to Competing Offers

- **O-4003** (Senior Data Scientist, $195K base vs. $210K ask): Declined for Anthropic — higher equity was the deciding factor. The $15K base gap plus perceived equity upside at a frontier AI company made Headway's offer uncompetitive.
- **O-4005** (Staff Frontend Engineer, $248K base vs. $260K ask): Declined over title/scope mismatch, not comp. Wanted a director-level title Headway could not offer.
- **O-4008** (People Business Partner, $142K vs. $150K ask): Lost to a director-level role at Gusto. Comp and title both fell short.
- **O-4012** (Engineering Manager, $225K vs. $240K ask): Wanted M2-level scope. Again, level/scope mismatch was the root cause.
- **O-4014** (Software Engineer II, $148K vs. $155K ask): Relocated and took a remote role. Lifestyle decision, not purely comp-driven.

**Pattern:** When candidates declined for comp-related reasons (O-4003, O-4008), the base shortfall was $8K–$15K. When the gap was small and the package included structural elements like signing bonuses, candidates accepted (O-4001 had a $10K base gap but a $20K signing bonus and accepted in 3 days).

### Structural Elements in Accepted Offers

| Element | Frequency in Accepted Offers | Avg. Amount |
|---------|------------------------------|-------------|
| Signing Bonus | 10 of 10 accepted offers included one | $10,800 |
| Equity Grant | 10 of 10 | $56,600 avg |
| Days to Decision | Accepted offers averaged | 3.3 days |
| Declined offers averaged | — | 8.6 days |

**Notable patterns:**
- Every single accepted offer included a signing bonus (range: $5K–$20K). This was table stakes, not a differentiator.
- Faster decisions (2–5 days) correlated perfectly with acceptances. All offers that took 6+ days were declined.
- No accepted offers used accelerated vesting, but the equity grants were generally positioned at or above band midpoints.

## 4. Final Recommendation

**Recommended Scenario: B (Competitive) with one adjustment — increase base to $195,000.**

Offer the candidate $195,000 base, $90,000 equity (4-year vest, 1-year cliff), 12% bonus target ($23,400), and a $15,000 signing bonus, for a Year-1 total compensation of approximately $256,150 plus the signing bonus. This positions the base at roughly the 55th market percentile and $17K above the competing Series C offer, creating a clear and decisive gap without approaching band max. The recent offers data is unambiguous: when Headway meets or modestly exceeds the candidate ask on base and includes a signing bonus, the acceptance rate is 100% and decisions come in under five days. A $195K base is $10K above the candidate's stated expectation, which creates a positive surprise effect while staying $12K below band midpoint — preserving room for Year-1 merit adjustments and maintaining internal equity with other IC4 Product hires. The signing bonus gives the candidate a tangible, immediate reason to say yes today rather than shop the offer. This package wins the candidate without setting a precedent that every competing offer triggers a top-of-band response.
